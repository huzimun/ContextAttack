
# ContextAttack: Adversarial Image Attacks on Diffusion Transformer-Based Image Editing

## 🔍 About
Recent advances in diffusion-based image editing models, particularly large-scale systems built upon Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) and flow matching objectives, have significantly improved the fidelity, scalability, and contextual controllability of visual content generation. However, these developments also raise critical safety concerns, especially regarding the unintended or malicious generation of Not Safe for Work (NSFW) content. Prior work has shown that adversarial text prompts can induce such behavior, yet these prompts are often detectable by existing text-based filtering mechanisms, limiting their effectiveness. Moreover, existing adversarial image attacks on image editing models have primarily focused on traditional UNet-based diffusion architectures, leaving the security of modern DiT-based editing frameworks largely unexplored.

In this paper, we identify a previously overlooked vulnerability: adversarial image attacks targeting Diffusion Transformer-based image editing systems under the flow matching paradigm. We propose **ContextAttack**, a novel framework that perturbs input images to manipulate contextual representations within the editing process, thereby inducing the model to generate NSFW content without modifying the accompanying text prompts. Unlike prior approaches designed for UNet-based models, our method explicitly exploits the global context modeling and token-level interactions inherent in DiT architectures, enabling more effective and transferable attacks. By optimizing imperceptible perturbations, ContextAttack successfully bypasses existing safety mechanisms, including latent-space filtering strategies, while preserving high visual fidelity of adversarial inputs.

Extensive experiments demonstrate that our approach consistently compromises multiple state-of-the-art DiT-based image editing models across diverse settings. These findings expose fundamental security risks in emerging flow matching-based editing systems and highlight the urgent need for robust defense mechanisms tailored to next-generation diffusion architectures.
